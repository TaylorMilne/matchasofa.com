The MATCHA revolutionizes the livingroom making it a more Open, Zen, Modern and Functional space.

It organically converts into a Ottoman, Memory foam bed, Bamboo table, Chaise lounge, workstation. That is all included in the standard unit. Additional add-ons are available to go even further. We call it Multi-functional.

We've used eco-friendly products such as Bamboo wood, bamboo foam and Ramie fibre. These materials are not only sustainable, but also have superior properties of their synthetic/non-sustainable siblings.

Because of it's modularity, you can change your living room setup in seconds. This will make it feel like a new apartment everyday.

Shape your living room to make you, or your guests, feel truly welcome. You can now host any event right at home.

With MATCHA, You are now the designer.

Trevor

Trevor has spent the last 20 years contracting for the Canadian Feature Film, Commercial & Television Industry. In the beginning he worked as a carpenter building and constructing all types of sets and props for commercial television.

He was quickly snapped up by management and adapted to the environment. After years of working as an assistant director, he now works more closely with the designers and production management coordinating the on location aspect of filmmaking. Working closely with the producers from all over the world,Trevor has a keen ability to develop relationships and has been involved with countless marketing & viral marketing schemes for all sorts of products.

With out of the box thinking, focus and endless creative drive to improve & simplify, he is a great addition to the company. Together, we solidified the design and engineering. We also visualized our branding concept and sales strategies.

Julian

Danish designer, Julian Jorgensen, was born and raised in Billund, the town where Lego first started, and subsequently both his parents were part of the design & marketing team at Lego—influencing his creative mind at a very young age and for years to come.

During his many years of travelling in Asia, he became more and more inspired by Japanese design. He'd visit several Zen temples in Kyoto, and was in love with the empty rooms, the low seating, and the tatami mat flooring.

As he worked & traveled he became an admirer of elegance & simplicity of Japanese furniture, the truth he realized is that all to often they’re uncomfortable —mistakes made by many others, function is lost in the artistic appeal.

In 2010 he had had enough combining ideas from past present and future it is of no surprise that years later, he designed a modular sofa concept that is portable, multifunctional, simplistic, & comfortable for various types of people in various types of environments.

The fabric is made from 100% Ramie fibre grown locally in China. Died with natural colours.

The foam used is a mix of Bamboo Charcoal Foam, Memory foam and PU foam.

All wood is made from eco-friendly bamboo harvested in local province.
